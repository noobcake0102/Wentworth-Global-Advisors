Frequently Asked

Common questions about fractional executive services.

What does a fractional COO do?

A fractional COO operates as your part-time chief operating officer. They run the operations function — production, supply chain, quality, KPI accountability, and team structure — without the cost or commitment of a full-time hire. Wentworth Global Advisors provides fractional COO services to manufacturing companies that need senior operational leadership for a finite window: post-acquisition, during a turnaround, while a permanent COO is being recruited, or to scale production through a critical contract ramp.

When should I hire an interim CEO for a turnaround company?

If on-time delivery has fallen below 80%, customers are escalating to ownership, or you cannot explain why margins are slipping, an interim CEO can stabilize the business while a permanent search runs. Wentworth specializes in interim CEO for turnaround companies in manufacturing — particularly aerospace, defense, satellite, and high-reliability electronics — where the cost of getting it wrong is measured in lost contracts, not just lost quarters.

How is a fractional COO different from a management consultant?

A management consultant gives you a recommendation and leaves. A fractional COO is accountable for the result. We don’t deliver decks — we run the operation. We sit in the executive seat, own the P&L, lead the team, and stay until the numbers move. That’s the difference between an operations consultant for private equity and a true executive operations advisor.

Do you support post-merger integration?

Yes. Post-merger integration is a core practice. We lead end-to-end M&A integration services for operations, including headcount, IT, culture, product, and facility rationalization. Engagements typically run 3–9 months. We work most often with small and mid-sized private equity buyers integrating manufacturing acquisitions where operational performance is part of the value-creation thesis.

What industries does Wentworth Global Advisors serve?

Aerospace and defense manufacturing ($20M–$200M), commercial aerospace and defense adjacencies in the LEO and MEO satellite industry and other high-tech, high-reliability industries ($10M–$600M), and various industrial design, manufacturing, and warehousing businesses ($2M–$20M). If your company makes physical things and they have to work the first time, every time, we know your problems.

Where are you based and do you travel?

We are based in Marco Island, Southwest Florida, and serve clients across the United States and internationally. Most engagements blend on-site immersion during the diagnosis and stabilization phase with remote leadership thereafter. We deliberately structure ourselves as a remote fractional executive service so the right operator can run the right business, regardless of zip code.
