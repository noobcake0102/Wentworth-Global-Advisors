The Pattern

Different industries. The same diagnosis sequence.

Across these three engagements — aerospace metallurgy, defense electronics, and satellite ground stations — the path to operational turnaround for an underperforming company followed the same disciplined sequence. We document it here because pattern recognition is what separates an executive operations advisor from a generalist consultant.

  1. Diagnose at the floor level, not in the conference room. Talk to engineers about their X-input variables. Look at the wiring on the machine. Read the actual contract spec. The diagnosis is in the operation, not the executive summary.
  2. Stabilize the customer relationship first. If customers are escalating, no internal fix lands. Build the relationship, present the recovery commitment yourself, and protect the revenue line.
  3. Reset the production system. SMED, balanced schedule, single-piece flow, stockroom redesign, configuration control, and SPC. These are the levers. They work in metallurgy, electronics, and satellite hardware alike.
  4. Rebuild design discipline. Integrated Product Development for producibility and testability. Environmental qualification where commercial specs were silent. Specifications written so customers can’t reject in test.
  5. Hand it back — and leave. The successful engagement ends with the existing team running the operation without us. That is the difference between a turnaround and a dependency.
